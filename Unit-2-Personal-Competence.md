## ***Unit-2***

### *Personal Competence Personal Competence*

*Personal competence in EI refers to the ability to understand and manage one's own emotions effectively*

*It encompasses two key components*

1. *Self-Management*  
2. *Self-Awareness*

---  

### *Self-management* 
- *Self-management is the skill of controlling emotions, impulses and actions in various situations.*
- *It involves handling stress, adjusting to changes and staying positive.*
- *Self-management also means being aware of your emotions and being aware of your emotions and using simple strategies like deep breathing to stay calm and focused.*
- *Developing self management skills help individuals handle challenging situations calmly and constructively, instead of reacting emotionally.*
- *It includes setting and achieving personal and professional goals, staying organized and disciplined, and showing resilience during setbacks.*

---

### *Self-Awareness*
- *Self-awareness is the foundation of EI* 
- *It means understanding your own emotions, strengths, weaknesses, values, and what drives you.*
- *It helps you recognize how you feel and why you feel that way. Knowing yourself better allows you to make smarter decisions and improve your relationships.* 
- *It means being aware of your thoughts, feelings, and reactions without judging or defending them.*
- *Self-awareness allows understanding their emotional state and its impact on their thoughts and behaviors.*
- *It involves understanding personal triggers, patterns of behavior, and emotional biases.*
  
---

### *Conclusion* 

*Developing self-management and self-awareness skills is important for personal growth, emotional well-being and building healthy relationships. When individuals learn to manage their emotions and understand themselves better, they can handle challenges more effectively, strengthen their connections with others, and make smarter decisions. These skills can be improved through simple practices like self-reflection, observing one's behavior, asking for feedback, and using techniques such as mindfulness and emotional control exercises.*
